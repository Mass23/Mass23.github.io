The-always-series


In social sciences, intersectionality serves as a framework aiming to elucidate the intricate interplay among factors such as race, gender, class, and various identity markers, in shaping the intricate patterns of oppression and domination. In this artwork, each element of the quadriptych shows GDP and wealth data overlayed with a line symbolically depicting the reduction in glaciers' area since the 1970s. Through this visual representation, the artwork links one of the most conspicuous effect of climate change with multiple indicators associated with capitalism and disparities. In doing so, it conceptually portrays the intersectionality inherent in the roles of economy, colonialism, and greed, in collectively driving the ongoing climate crisis.

Programmatic images created using data of GDP, glacier surface area and the share of the total net worth held by the top 1%. The graphs were produced using the R programming language and the ggplot2, tidyverse and showtext packages.
