A-glitch-in-the-ethics


A-glitch-in-the-ethics explores the aesthetic and conceptual parallels between computational glitches and systemic failures in the real world. By combining programmatic, data-driven processes with glitch-art techniques, the project highlights the distortions, erasures, and ruptures that occur when ethical frameworks collapse. The visual language of glitches—fragmentation, corruption, disintegration—serves as both metaphor and evidence for the violence embedded in global structures.

Referencing ongoing atrocities, such as the genocide of Palestinians in Gaza, the work draws a direct line between digital error and human destruction. In this context, the glitch is no longer a technical malfunction, but a reflection of ethical breakdowns that are neither accidental nor isolated, but designed and maintained. The project stands as a testimony to the current state of ethical collapse, questioning the aesthetic distance that allows destruction to be witnessed, yet rarely confronted.
